# **EarnLearning: Brand & Design Guide**

## **Introduction**

This document outlines the core visual identity for EarnLearning. Our brand is built on a foundation of empowerment, innovation, and professional growth. We are equipping the next generation of female leaders in tech and business with the skills to succeed. Our identity must reflect this mission: it is modern, trustworthy, and energetic, yet sophisticated and professional—never childish.

This guide will ensure consistency, clarity, and impact across all platform touchpoints.

## **1. Color Palette**

## **2. Typography**

Our typography is clean, modern, and highly legible, ensuring a comfortable reading experience for both English and Korean text. We will use Google Fonts for their accessibility and ease of implementation.

### **Headings & UI Elements**
**Font:** **Plus Jakarta Sans**

- **Why:** A versatile and modern geometric sans-serif. It feels tech-forward and clean, with excellent clarity for headlines, buttons, and navigation menus. Its multiple weights allow for a strong visual hierarchy.
- **Usage:**
    - H1, H2, H3: Bold (700), Semi-Bold (600)
    - Buttons, Tabs, Navigation: Medium (500), Semi-Bold (600)

### **Body & Paragraph Text**
**Font:** **Noto Sans KR**

- **Why:** Essential for a platform targeting a Korean audience. It provides impeccable rendering of Korean characters (Hangul) while also offering excellent readability for Latin characters (English). This ensures a seamless bilingual experience.
- **Usage:**
    - Body copy, descriptions, long-form text: Regular (400)
    - Captions, helper text: Light (300)

### **Code Snippets & Data**
**Font:** **JetBrains Mono**

- **Why:** A typeface created specifically for developers. It's highly legible, with distinct characters (e.g., `l` vs `1`, `O` vs `0`) and is optimized for reading code on screens. This is crucial for the coding education aspect of the platform.
- **Usage:**
    - All code blocks, terminal outputs, and numerical data displays (like stock prices).

## **3. Icon & Logo Style**

Our visual elements should be as clean and purposeful as our interface.

### **Logo Style**
The EarnLearning logo should be a **combination mark** (a symbol paired with a logotype). The symbol should be abstract and geometric, suggesting concepts like growth, connection, or a rising stock chart. The logotype should use a customized version of our heading font, **Plus Jakarta Sans**.

### **Iconography Style**
- **Style:** **Line Icons (Stroked)**
- **Weight:** Use a consistent stroke weight (e.g., 1.5px or 2px). This creates a light, modern, and sophisticated feel.
- **Details:** Icons should have rounded corners and terminals to feel more approachable and friendly. Avoid sharp, aggressive edges.
- **Interaction:** On hover or in an active state, icons can transition to a **filled** version or increase in stroke weight to provide clear visual feedback.
- **Recommendation:** Use a high-quality, consistent library like **Phosphor Icons** or **Feather Icons** as a base.

## **4. UI Design Principles**

These principles will guide the design and user experience of the EarnLearning platform.

1.  **Clarity Above All:** The interface must be intuitive. Students are here to learn, not to learn how to use the platform. Use ample whitespace, clear visual hierarchy, and concise language. Every element should have a clear purpose.

2.  **Gamification with Purpose:** Gamified elements (currency, stocks, leaderboards) should directly relate to and reinforce learning objectives. Visualize progress and financial data with clean charts and graphs, not distracting or childish animations.

3.  **Encourage, Don't Distract:** The design should feel motivating. Use **Momentum Coral** to celebrate wins and highlight calls to action. Progress bars, checklists, and positive feedback messages should guide the user forward.

4.  **Data-Driven & Professional:** The "company" and "stock market" features should be presented with clean, professional data visualizations. This reinforces the real-world applicability of the skills being taught and respects the intelligence of the university-level audience.

5.  **Responsive & Accessible:** Design with a mobile-first mindset. Ensure all text and interactive elements meet WCAG AA contrast standards. Our color palette has been chosen with this in mind.
